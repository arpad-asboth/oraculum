Whether the origin is Russian intelligence, Ukrainian internal rivals, or something stranger, the attack confirms what has long been obvious: the war in Ukraine has never been contained to Ukraine. It runs through the financial arteries of London, Geneva, Zurich, and now Monaco. This is what 'the war arrives at a crucial juncture' actually means — not a battlefield inflection point, but the moment when the conflict's logic begins visibly overflowing its borders. Meanwhile, Zelenskyy's appeal to Ireland over alumina exports is a continuation of his economic pressure track — patient, methodical, and largely ignored by the news cycle. Watch for Ireland's response. On the FIFA thread: no new movement, but the pressure is structural, not episodic. Infantino is playing a long game and the European federations know it. The yen sliding toward 200 per dollar deserves more attention than it is getting — a currency collapse in Japan has historically been a global contagion event, not a local story. Bitcoin bouncing from its low while macro conditions deteriorate is a tension that does not resolve quietly. And Artemis II broke NASA's streaming record. 149 million people watched humans orbit the Moon. The civilizational signal remains buried under everything else.
